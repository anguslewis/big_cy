Deep, standalone survey: convenience yields, the zero-beta rate, safe-asset-demand
models of the international financial system, CY mechanisms, and relative-vs-absolute
measurement. Surveyed **broadly and neutrally** — not oriented around any single
model or mechanism.

## The four strands

1. **Zero-beta rate & the ABSOLUTE level** of convenience (DiTella-Hébert-Kurlat-Wang
   and rivals; how the level is identified; what is contested).
2. **International models** with safe-asset / convenience demand (Kekre-Lenel; JKL;
   Farhi-Maggiori; Maggiori; Gourinchas-Ray-Vayanos; Caballero-Farhi-Gourinchas;
   He-Krishnamurthy-Milbradt; Devereux-Engel-Wu; Koijen-Yogo; Bianchi-Bigio-Engel; …).
3. **Mechanisms & microfoundations** (MIU/BiU; safety/collateral/information-insensitivity;
   public-debt-as-liquidity; search; regulatory; KVJ aggregate demand; reduced-form vs
   deep-microfounded).
4. **Relative-vs-absolute measurement** (CIP basis; Du-Im-Schreger; Treasury–AAA;
   swapped/TIPS spreads; option-box and zero-beta absolute measures; what each identifies).

## The one-sentence takeaway

Relative measures (CIP basis, Treasury–AAA, Treasury premium ≈ tens of bp) net out
convenience common to both legs and so cannot recover the level; the two *absolute*
measures disagree by an order of magnitude (option-box ≈ 40 bp vs. zero-beta-equity
≈ 2–4%); and no structural open-economy model has yet asked whether a
DiTella-magnitude *absolute* convenience yield can do the work currently done by
risk-bearing heterogeneity in Kekre-Lenel — the cell big_cy occupies.
